Bio

I am a junior student majoring in Computer Science in Wuhan University, expected to graduate in 2024. Currently, I am a Research Assistant at MIT-IBM Watson AI Lab and UMass Amherst, advised by Prof. Chuang Gan & Dr. Zhenfang Chen. Previously, I am a Research Assistant at Wuhan University, advised by Prof. Lefei Zhang & Prof. Zuchao Li.

My research interests can be summarized by three key concepts: logic, nature, and humanity. In my perspective, logic is the crucial link between natural and artificial attributes. The overarching goal of my research is to build a human-like machine, that can perceive the world, reason inside the model, and interact with people using human language. Currently, I focus on the intersection of Computer Vision and Natural Language Processing, and how to endow AI models with reasoning abilities.
