RESEARCH

My research interests can be summarized by three key concepts: logic, nature, and humanity. I want to understand human cognition. We humans learn from nature, and internalize it through their thinking and reasoning. This cognitive process is so fast, and works with so little data. Key to this process is human logic, serving as the link between natural and artificial attributes. But why do humans seem to have innate learning and reasoning ability? What can machine algorithms imitate from human cognitive processes? How can multimodal data aid another modality to learn in a more human-like manner, instead of simply merging them together? These questions are what I am most interested in.

Specifically, these three concepts can be mapped to some common topics, like logic to reasoning, nature to vision and physics, and humanity to language, robotics and human-centered AI. Currently, I am focusing on how to endow vision and language models with reasoning abilities. The overarching goal of my AI research is to build a human-level machine, which can perceive the world via sensory inputs like video, reason and plan inside a unified model, and interact with people using human languages. In fact, I am very interested in many disciplines, including cognitive science, psychology, mathematics, brain science, and sociology.
