Bio

I am a senior student majoring in Computer Science in Wuhan University, expected to graduate in 2024. Currently, I am a Research Assistant at MIT-IBM Watson AI Lab, advised by Prof. Chuang Gan & Dr. Zhenfang Chen. Previously, I was a Research Assistant at Wuhan University, advised by Prof. Lefei Zhang & Prof. Zuchao Li.

My research interests can be summarized by three key concepts: logic, nature, and humanity. I want to understand human cognition. We humans learn from nature, and translate it into knowledge in our minds through thinking and reasoning. So I think logic is the crucial link between natural and artificial attributes. Currently, I am learning about Artificial Intelligence, and researching on how to bring machine intelligence closer to the ability of human intelligence. In fact, I am very interested in many disciplines, including cognitive science, psychology, mathematics, brain science, and sociology.

Specifically, I am now focusing on the intersection of Computer Vision and Natural Language Processing, and how to endow AI models with reasoning abilities. The overarching goal of my AI research is to build a human-level machine, that can perceive the world via sensory inputs like video, reason and plan inside a unified model, and interact with people using human language.
